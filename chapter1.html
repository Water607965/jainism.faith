Chapter 1: Introduction to Jainism

Jainism is one of the oldest religions in the world, emphasizing a path of non-violence (Ahimsa), truth, and self-discipline. Unlike many religious traditions that depend on a creator god, Jainism is an **atheistic religion** focused on achieving liberation (Moksha) through self-realization.

Origins of Jainism

Jainism traces its roots to the **Indus Valley Civilization (3300–1300 BCE)** and later evolved through the teachings of **24 Tirthankaras (spiritual teachers)**. The first Tirthankara was **Rishabhanatha**, while the most well-known is **Mahavira (599–527 BCE)**, who refined the Jain doctrine.

Key Teachings

  • Ahimsa (Non-Violence): The fundamental principle of Jainism, extending beyond actions to thoughts and speech.
  • Aparigraha (Non-Possessiveness): Encouraging detachment from material goods.
  • Anekantavada (Many-Sided Reality): Accepting that no single viewpoint represents absolute truth.

Jainism's Influence on the World

Jainism has significantly influenced **Indian philosophy**, **vegetarianism**, and **environmental ethics**. Figures like **Mahatma Gandhi** adopted its core teachings, particularly Ahimsa.
